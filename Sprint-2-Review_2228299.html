Travel Booking System : Sprint 2 Review

Created by Anushka Shetty, last modified on Jun 05, 2023

What went well? 

  • We improved our task planning compared to the previous sprint. By identifying interdependencies between tasks in advance, we were able to allocate work more effectively and avoid bottlenecks. This allowed team members to work in parallel, resulting in smoother progress.

  • We made a conscious effort to keep our SCRUM meetings concise and focused. By avoiding excessive discussions and staying on topic, we were able to maximize the efficiency of our meetings. This allowed us to address any roadblocks promptly and make timely decisions.

  • Regular SCRUM meetings facilitated early and frequent communication among team members. During these meetings, team members could share any roadblocks they faced, allowing us to identify issues early on and resolve them before they escalated.  

  • During Sprint 2, we adhered to the planned delivery dates to ensure the timely completion of tasks instead of deferring them to the next sprint.  

What should we stop doing? 

  • A few team members were late to SCRUM meetings. Avoiding this would have prevented other team members from being forced to wait, thus making the meetings more efficient. 

  • Avoiding discussions in SCRUM meetings and keep them short. 

  • Some of the code changes were overwritten, developing on the outdated code should be avoided. 

What could have done better? 

  • While we made progress in updating task statuses on Jira, there were instances where the updates were not as accurate or timely as desired. To enhance project tracking and transparency, we need to ensure that all team members consistently provide precise updates on task statuses.

  • Although our communication during SCRUM meetings improved, there is still room for enhancement. We should strive to foster an environment where team members actively share their challenges, progress, and ideas beyond the meetings.

  • While we improved our planning regarding task interdependencies, there were still some instances where dependencies caused delays or hindered progress. Going forward, we need to continue refining our dependency management to minimize such issues and optimize our development process.

What should we start doing? 

  • It is important to report any issues that team members face, particularly those related to time and development, such as difficulty in completing tasks within the allocated time frame.  

  • To avoid excessive discussions during SCRUM meetings, team members should provide an overview of their current progress and highlight any challenges they are facing before the meeting. 

  • We will establish clear guidelines and expectations for updating task statuses on Jira. Regular and accurate updates will be emphasized to maintain transparency and aid project tracking.
